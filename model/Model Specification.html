1. Purpose and scope of this specification

This document specifies the PMS-ANTICIPATION layer in a concise, technical form. It is based on the YAML file PMS-ANTICIPATION.yaml (with schema_version = "PMS-ANTICIPATION_1.1") and renders its structure, constraints, and semantics explicit for human readers and computational systems.

PMS-ANTICIPATION is an application profile (overlay) of the Praxeological Meta-Structure (PMS; Δ–Ψ). It does not redefine PMS operators, dependencies, layers, or derived axes. Instead, it formalizes anticipation as a present structural stance: a disciplined handling of possible futures and non-events under asymmetry (Ω) and irreversibility (Θ), without closure claims or action authorization.

The specification also makes explicit that this overlay does not claim monopoly over anticipation analysis. Rival framings may address other dimensions, and not all anticipatory configurations must translate cleanly into PMS operator chains.

The specification covers, in particular:

  • the schema_meta block (identity, status, authorship, explicit dependency on PMS_1.3, and methodological restraint);
  • the validity gate enforcing Χ (Distance), reversibility, and D (Dignity-in-Practice);
  • the anticipation lens definition and operator role mappings (Δ–Ψ → anticipation functions);
  • reduced signatures defining minimal and viable anticipatory configurations;
  • viability criteria and formal invalidity markers;
  • contrast frames (anticipation vs prognosis);
  • configuration comparison and structural trade-offs;
  • structural drift patterns and post-confirmation discipline;
  • scope limitations, rival framings, and non-capture openness;
  • a minimal application protocol (MAP) and example schema.
Core idea

PMS-ANTICIPATION models anticipation not as future knowledge, prediction, or justification for intervention, but as a self-binding discipline of restraint that remains structurally viable even when anticipated events do not occur — and is tested most sharply when they do.

Conceptual separation

PMS defines the operator grammar. PMS-ANTICIPATION defines how that grammar is used to keep the future open without turning probability into authority or restraint into covert dominance.

3. Validity gate and scope constraints

3.1 Application gate

  • Χ (Distance): stop-capability must remain active even under high probability.
  • Reversibility: all anticipatory readings are scene-bound and revisable.
  • D (Dignity-in-Practice): anticipation must not coerce, shame, rank, or authorize dominance.

3.2 Explicit non-goals

  • prediction or future truth claims;
  • action mandates or preparedness imperatives;
  • moralization of uncertainty;
  • externalization of self-binding (Ψ → others);
  • treating anticipation as exhaustive of all future-oriented analysis.
Key firewall

If anticipation is used to justify intervention, closure, or coercive authority, it has become formally invalid within PMS application criteria.

4. Anticipation as a structural configuration

Configuration Signature Meaning
Minimal anticipation Δ + □ + Λ + Θ + Χ A bounded possibility remains open as a non-event under irreversibility, with sustained restraint.
Viable anticipation Δ → ∇ → □ → Λ → Α → Ω → Θ → Φ → Χ → Σ → Ψ Impulse is regulated, patterns recognized, asymmetry handled, openness integrated, and restraint self-bound over time.
Downstream planning constraint Χ + D + reversibility Any planning that follows anticipation remains constrained by restraint, dignity-in-practice, and non-premature closure.
Non-equivalence

Anticipation is not prognosis, not planning, and not readiness. These may occur downstream, but anticipation itself authorizes none of them.

5. Viability criteria, prognosis contrast, and structural trade-off

5.1 Positive viability criteria

  • Λ carried: openness remains active rather than resolved.
  • Θ respected: irreversibility and delayed feedback are acknowledged.
  • Χ active: high probability does not become authority.
  • Ψ binds self: obligation is not externalized onto others.
  • Σ integrates openness: uncertainty is coordinated without false certainty.

5.2 Formal invalidity markers

  • Λ replaced by certainty
  • Θ neutralized by action compulsion
  • Χ suspended
  • Ω instrumentalized
  • Ψ externalized

5.3 Anticipation vs prognosis

Prognosis may provide descriptive material, but within PMS criteria it becomes formally invalid when it supplies authority by closing Λ, suspending Χ, or eroding D. Anticipation, by contrast, is a present structural stance that remains viable under non-occurrence.

5.4 Structural trade-off

The overlay distinguishes two recurrent configuration tendencies: an action-proximate pattern and a restraint-oriented pattern. Effectiveness tends to increase as Ψ binds toward E, whereas stability tends to increase as Χ precedes E. PMS-ANTICIPATION treats these as structurally incompatible optimizations, not as defects corrigible simply by better information.

6. Structural drift, misuse corridors, and post-confirmation discipline

PMS-ANTICIPATION treats confirmation as a second-order stress test. Being right increases the obligation to restore Χ, not to abandon it.

  • Preemptive authoritarian drift: Ω inflation under anticipation.
  • Probability-induced action compulsion: likelihood → must-act logic.
  • Moralization of uncertainty: Λ becomes normative warrant.
  • Preventive escalation: irreversible commitments before Λ is resolved.
  • AD_A≫E: evaluative expansion with chronically delayed enactment.
  • Ψ overbinding: identity bound to correctness after confirmation.
  • Λ rewrite: prior uncertainty retroactively denied.
Structural warning

The most dangerous error is using anticipation to legitimate intervention rather than to bind oneself to restraint.

7. Responsibility, dignity, scope limits, and rival framings

7.1 Responsibility and dignity

Within this overlay, anticipation does not authorize action; it deepens responsibility for restraint. Responsibility attaches only where effective, frame-bound influence exists, and remains viable only if Χ stays active under Θ and Ω.

Dignity-in-Practice is preserved only where anticipation does not amplify threat narratives into inevitability, does not justify coercion, and does not replace present justification with imported prior expectations.

7.2 Scope limitations

  • Epistemic anticipation addresses future-state estimation.
  • Phenomenological anticipation lies outside PMS by design.
  • Strategic anticipation may overlap in cases, but strategic success is not a PMS criterion.

7.3 Genuine non-capture

The overlay explicitly preserves the possibility that some anticipatory configurations resist clean translation into PMS operator chains. Coherent translation alone does not prove exhaustive capture.

7.4 Complementarity

PMS-ANTICIPATION is not presented as the only legitimate analysis of anticipation. It is strongest where it makes a specific structural dimension visible that predictive, strategic, or precautionary framings may not foreground.

8. Minimal Application Protocol (MAP)

  1. □ Frame check: Where does influence actually exist?
  2. Λ Non-event integrity: What might not happen?
  3. Ω Asymmetry audit: Who bears irreversible cost?
  4. Χ Distance enforcement: Where is urgency replacing structure?
  5. Ψ Self-binding test: Who is actually bound?
Invalidity marker

“Others must act now” = Ψ-externalization and therefore formal invalidity within PMS application criteria.

8.1 Red-flag patterns

  • We must act because… → Λ collapsed
  • The data clearly shows… → Α → certainty
  • Responsibility demands intervention → Ω instrumentalized
  • Delay is irresponsible → Χ suspended
  • They should have known → retroactive guilt

8.2 What this overlay can and cannot do

Can: make structural drift visible, track Λ/Ω/Θ/Χ interactions, distinguish self-binding from other-binding, and show when anticipation becomes coercive or prematurely closed.

Cannot: supply action mandates, evaluate success by predictive accuracy alone, replace other anticipatory frameworks, or decide when action must occur in non-PMS terms.
